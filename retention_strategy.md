## 1. Champions

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers purchase frequently, spend the most, and have the lowest churn rate. They are the most valuable group.

**Retention Action**
Provide loyalty rewards, early access to products, and exclusive benefits.

**Expected Business Value**
Retaining Champions protects the highest revenue-generating customers.

---

## 2. Loyal Customers

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers purchase regularly and have moderate churn risk. Many have the potential to become Champions.

**Retention Action**
Use product recommendations, free shipping offers, and loyalty enrollment campaigns.

**Expected Business Value**
Increasing purchase frequency can move more customers into the Champions segment.

---

## 3. High-Value Unhappy

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers spend well but have raised multiple support tickets or show negative sentiment.

**Retention Action**
Resolve support issues first and follow up with personalized communication.

**Expected Business Value**
Improving customer satisfaction can prevent the loss of high-value customers.

---

## 4. At-Risk

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers were active in the past but have not purchased recently. Churn risk is very high.

**Retention Action**
Run win-back campaigns with personalized offers and reminders.

**Expected Business Value**
Recovering even a small percentage of this segment can generate significant revenue.

---

## 5. Dormant

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers show very little recent activity and have the highest churn rate.

**Retention Action**
Send one final re-engagement campaign through email, SMS, or app notifications.

**Expected Business Value**
Recovery potential is low, so spending should remain limited.

---

## 6. Discount-Sensitive

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers mainly purchase when discounts are available and have low average spending.

**Retention Action**
Use bundle offers and targeted promotions instead of frequent discounts.

**Expected Business Value**
Helps maintain sales while protecting profit margins.

---

## 7. New / Low-Engagement

**Customer Behaviour**
These customers have limited purchase history and nearly half eventually churn.

**Retention Action**
Use onboarding campaigns, product recommendations, and first-repeat-purchase incentives.

**Expected Business Value**
Converting these customers into repeat buyers can improve long-term retention.

---

## Campaign Budget Prioritization

| Priority | Segment              | Reason                                 |
| -------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| 1        | At-Risk              | High churn and high revenue potential  |
| 2        | New / Low-Engagement | Largest customer group                 |
| 3        | High-Value Unhappy   | Valuable customers with service issues |
| 4        | Loyal Customers      | Opportunity to create more Champions   |
| 5        | Champions            | Retention focused, low campaign cost   |
| 6        | Discount-Sensitive   | Lower spending customers               |
| 7        | Dormant              | Lowest recovery potential              |


The main focus should be on At-Risk, New/Low-Engagement, and High-Value Unhappy customers because they offer the greatest opportunity to reduce churn and improve revenue.
